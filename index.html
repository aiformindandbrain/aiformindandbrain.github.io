Foundation Models for Mind and Brain

NeurIPS 2025 Workshop

Foundation models, such as large language models (LLMs) and vision language models (VLMs), have demonstrated remarkable capabilities across multiple AI/ML domains, including vision, language, and robotics. Moreover, with the recent inference-time scaling paradigm, a new type of foundation model, large reasoning models, have shown tremendous promise in achieving strong reasoning abilities in a broad range of challenging tasks. In this workshop, we will explore an emerging use of these foundation models: as powerful tools for understanding and modeling the mind (cognition) and the brain (neuroscience). Research in this new, exciting direction will have a far-reaching impact on (1) the science of intelligence, (2) cognitive science/neuroscience inspired AI, and (3) cooperative and human-centered AI systems.

Focusing on these three topics, this workshop will bring together neuroscience, cognitive science and machine learning researchers to examine how these models can inform and be informed by biological, psychological and neural theories.
