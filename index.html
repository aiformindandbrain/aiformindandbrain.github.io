Foundation Models for Mind and Brain

NeurIPS 2025 Workshop

About the workshop

Foundation models, such as large language models (LLMs) and vision language models (VLMs), have demonstrated remarkable capabilities across multiple AI/ML domains, including vision, language, and robotics. Moreover, with the recent inference-time scaling paradigm, a new type of foundation model, large reasoning models, have shown tremendous promise in achieving strong reasoning abilities in a broad range of challenging tasks. In this workshop, we will explore an emerging use of these foundation models: as powerful tools for understanding and modeling the mind (cognition) and the brain (neuroscience). Research in this new, exciting direction will have a far-reaching impact on (1) the science of intelligence, (2) cognitive science/neuroscience inspired AI, and (3) cooperative and human-centered AI systems.

Focusing on these three topics, this workshop will bring together neuroscience, cognitive science and machine learning researchers to examine how these models can inform and be informed by biological, psychological and neural theories.

Science of Intelligence

We will discuss how to advance the understanding and modeling of the mind (cognitive science) and brain (neuroscience). We will focus on the following questions:

  • How can we extract emergent cognitive skills, internal representations, and latent inductive biases in foundation models and compare them with human cognitive skills, neural representations, and cognitive biases?
  • How can we leverage foundation models to automatically discover cognitive and neural models for humans and animals?
  • Can large generative models help cognitive scientists and neuroscientists to procedurally generate high-quality and controllable stimuli for large-scale behavioral and neural studies?

Cognitive science and neuroscience inspired AI

We will explore ways in which new insights from cognitive science and neuroscience, enabled by foundation models, can better inform the development of future AI/ML models. Topics under this theme include, but are not limited to:

  • Aligning foundation models with behavioral and neural data, including both internal representations as well as cognitive abilities.
  • Cognitively and neurally inspired, reliable, interpretable, and steerable AI systems.
  • Human-AI benchmarking for studying the human-machine intelligence gaps.
  • Efficient test-time adaptation and continual learning inspired by human learning.

Cooperative and human-centered AI systems

Given our understanding of the human mind and brain, we will discuss how to engineer AI systems that can better understand and cooperate with humans in a trustworthy manner. Relevant topics for this theme include, but are not limited to:

  • Socially intelligent LLM agents that can successfully and robustly understand and interact with humans and other agents.
  • Adaptive AI systems for personalized assistance.
  • Interpretable and trustworthy AI systems enabled by foundation models that are more aligned with human cognitive skills and neural representations.
