Foundation Models for the Mind and Brain

NeurIPS 2025 Workshop

Foundation models, such as large language models (LLMs) and vision language models (VLMs), have demonstrated remarkable capabilities across multiple AI/ML domains, including vision, language, and robotics. Moreover, with the recent inference-time scaling paradigm, a new type of foundation model, large reasoning models, have shown tremendous promise in achieving strong reasoning abilities in a broad range of challenging tasks. In this workshop, we want to explore an emerging use of foundation models—as powerful tools for understanding and modeling the mind (cognition) and the brain (neuroscience). We envision that research toward this new, exciting direction could create far-reaching impact in (1) advancing the science of intelligence, (2) developing cognitive science/neuroscience inspired AI, and engineering cooperative and human-centered AI systems.

This workshop will bring together researchers from neuroscience, cognitive science and machine learning to examine how these models can inform and be informed by biological, psychological, and neural theories.

We will focus on three themes:

  • Science of Intelligence
  • Cognitive science and neuroscience inspired AI
  • Cooperative and human-centered AI systems
